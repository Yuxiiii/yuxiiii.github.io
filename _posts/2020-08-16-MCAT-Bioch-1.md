## 1.1 Amino Acids Found in Proteins

Amino acids are molecules that contain two functional groups: an **amino group(-NH<sub>2</sub>)** and an **carboxyl group(-COOH)**. And between them there is a carbon atom acting as a bridge to connect these two group called alpha-carbon. In addition to the amino and carboxyl groups, the alpha-carbon has two other groups attached on it: a **hydrogen** and a **side chain, or called R group**. The R group determine the properties of amino acids, and therefore their functions.

### STEREOCHEMISTRY of AMINO ACIDS

### STRUCTURES of the AMINO ACIDS
There are  several ways to classify amino acids. In this section, amino acids will be classified depending on the structures of their side chains.

With this classification rule, amino acids can be divied into the following parts:
- Nonpolar, Nonaromatic Side Chain 
- Aromatic Side Chains
- Polar Side Chains
- Negaticely Charged (Acidic) Side Chains
- Postively CHarger (Basic) Side Chains

#### Nonpolar, Nonaromatic Side Chain
In this class, there are seven amino acids. 

**Methioine** is one of only two amino acids that contains a sulfur atom in its side chain. However, there is a methyl group attaching on the sulfur atom, which makes it could be consider as a relative nonpolar molecule.

![Methioine](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghuxbtwoj0j303804sgll.jpg)

**Proline** is unique in that it forms a cyclic amino acid. Unlike amino group in normal amino acids,in proline, amino nitrogen atom becomes a part of side chain, forming a five-membered ring. 

![Proline](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghuxb1sjmmj303f03bq2w.jpg)

And other five amino acids are aliphatic amino acids, which means it has a simple side chain. **Gycine**, mentioned before, only contains hydrogen in it's side chain. **Alanine, valine, leucine, and isoleucine**  have alkyl side chain containing one to four carbons.
