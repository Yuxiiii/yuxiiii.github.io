## 1.1 Amino Acids Found in Proteins

Amino acids are molecules that contain two functional groups: an **amino group(-NH<sub>2</sub>)** and an **carboxyl group(-COOH)**. And between them there is a carbon atom acting as a bridge to connect these two group called alpha-carbon. In addition to the amino and carboxyl groups, the alpha-carbon has two other groups attached on it: a **hydrogen** and a **side chain, or called R group**. The R group determine the properties of amino acids, and therefore their functions.

### STEREOCHEMISTRY of AMINO ACIDS

### STRUCTURES of the AMINO ACIDS
There are  several ways to classify amino acids. In this section, amino acids will be classified depending on the structures of their side chains.

With this classification rule, amino acids can be divied into the following parts:
- Nonpolar, Nonaromatic Side Chain 
- Aromatic Side Chains
- Polar Side Chains
- Negaticely Charged (Acidic) Side Chains
- Postively CHarger (Basic) Side Chains

#### Nonpolar, Nonaromatic Side Chains
In this class, there are seven amino acids. 

**Methioine** is one of only two amino acids that contains a sulfur atom in its side chain. However, there is a methyl group attaching on the sulfur atom, which makes it could be consider as a relative nonpolar molecule.

![Methioine](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghuxbtwoj0j303804sgll.jpg)

**Proline** is unique in that it forms a cyclic amino acid. Unlike amino group in normal amino acids,in proline, amino nitrogen atom becomes a part of side chain, forming a five-membered ring. 

![Proline](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghuxb1sjmmj303f03bq2w.jpg)

And other five amino acids are aliphatic amino acids, which means it has a simple side chain. **Gycine**, mentioned before, only contains hydrogen in it's side chain. **Alanine, valine, leucine, and isoleucine**  have alkyl side chain containing one to four carbons.

![Aliphatic amino acid](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghuygbnp0sj30i203z74n.jpg)

#### Aromatic Side Chains
Three amino acids have uncharge aromatic side chains. **Tryptophan** is the largest one with a double-ring system that contains a nitrogen atom. The smallest is **phenylalanine** with a benzyl ring plus a -CH<sub>2</sub>- group. And adding a hydorxyl group to phenylalanine would give the third memeber, **tyrosine**. 

While phenylalanine is relatively nonpolar, tyrosine is relatively polar due to the hydroxyl group.

![Aromatic amino acids](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghuypmxmpcj30a3069aae.jpg)

#### Polar Side Chains
Five amino acids have side chain that are polar but not aromatic. **Serine** and **Threonine** both have -OH groups in their side chains, which makes them highly polar and able to form hydrogen bonds.

![Serine](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghv8x23sh1j303703i0so.jpg) ![Threonine](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghv8x6a0uyj303603b748.jpg)

**Asparagine** and **Glutamine** have amide side chain. Unlike the amino group in the common amino acids, the amide nitrogen would not gain or loss the protons. Thus, they do not become charged.

![Asparagine and glutamine](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghv9h6wnyyj307004yglp.jpg)

**cystenine** has a thiol (-SH) group in side chain, which makes cysteine prine to oxidation.

![Cysteine](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghtpq7tibcj303e03lq2w.jpg)

#### Negatively Charged (Acidic) Side Chains
**Aspartic acid** and **glutamic acid** are only two negative charged amino acids in 20 amino acids. They have carboxylate groups in their side chains which can loss a proton to become a deprotonate form. 

![Aspartic acid and glutamic acid](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghv93jh03lj307c04njrh.jpg)

#### Positively Charged (Basic) Side Chains
The remaining three amino acids have side chain with positively charged nitrogen atoms. **Lysine** has a terminal amino group , while **arginine** three nitrogen atoms in its side chain. And **histidine** has an aromatic ring with two nitrogen atoms.

![Positively charged side chain](https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/007S8ZIlgy1ghva8g7xy7j309y0690t1.jpg)

### HYDROPHOBIC and HYDROPHILIC AMINO ACIDS

Firstly, the amino acids with alkyl side chains, such as alanine, valine, leucine, ioleucine, and phenylalanine are strongly hydrophobic and thus more likely to be found in the interior of proteins, away from water on the surface of the protein.

Secondly, all the amino acids with charged side chains including positively charged amino acids such as histidine, arginine, and lysine, plus negatively charged glutamate and aspartate are hydrophilic.
